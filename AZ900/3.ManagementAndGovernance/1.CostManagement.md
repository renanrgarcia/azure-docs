## TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)

- TCO is a financial estimate intended to help buyers and owners determine the direct and indirect costs of a product or system. In the context of cloud services, TCO includes:
  - **Initial costs**: Expenses related to the acquisition and setup of cloud resources.
  - **Operational costs**: Ongoing expenses for running and maintaining cloud services.
  - **Hidden costs**: Indirect costs that may not be immediately apparent, such as training, support, and downtime.

## Pricing Calculator

- The Azure Pricing Calculator is a tool that helps you estimate the cost of Azure services based on your specific requirements. You can use it to:
  - Select the Azure services you plan to use.
  - Configure the details of each service, such as region, instance size, and usage patterns.
  - View a detailed cost breakdown and estimate your monthly expenses.

## Azure Advisor

- Azure Advisor is a personalized cloud consultant that helps you follow best practices to optimize your Azure deployments. It analyzes your resource configuration and usage telemetry and provides recommendations in the following areas:
  - **High availability**: Ensure your applications are resilient and can recover from failures.
  - **Security**: Identify and mitigate potential security risks in your Azure environment.
  - **Performance**: Optimize your resources for better performance and efficiency.
  - **Cost**: Find opportunities to reduce costs and improve resource utilization.

## Azure Cost Management

- Azure Cost Management is a set of tools and features that help you monitor, allocate, and optimize your Azure spending. Key capabilities include:
  - **Cost analysis**: Gain insights into your spending patterns and identify areas for optimization.
  - **Budgets**: Set spending limits and receive alerts when you approach or exceed your budget.
  - **Recommendations**: Get personalized suggestions for reducing costs based on your usage patterns.

### Cost Alerts

- Cost Alerts allow you to set up notifications for your Azure spending. You can create alerts based on specific criteria, such as:
  - `Budget Alerts`: Get notified when you approach or exceed your budget limits.
  - `Credit Alerts`: Receive notifications when your Azure credits are running low.
  - `Department spending quota alerts`: Get notified when a specific department's spending exceeds its allocated budget.
